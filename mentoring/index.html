01 / How I can help

Practical help with the difficult part.

The session starts with the real system, decision, or failure—not a generic presentation.

01

Integration review

We map how applications, databases, files, and external services exchange data: what starts the flow, where data changes, who owns each step, and what happens when one dependency fails.

You leave withA dependency map, clear responsibilities, and a practical integration plan.
02

Legacy modernization plan

Before: every change feels risky and a full rewrite looks like the only option. After: we identify the smallest safe improvements, the tests required before release, and a way back if something fails.

You leave withWhat to change first, what to leave alone, and how to verify the result.
03

Engineering problem-solving

Bring a concrete design, debugging, automation, or production problem. We compare realistic options, failure paths, and operational consequences. Sensitive AI-assisted actions remain subject to human review and approval.

You leave withA decision you understand—not instructions you must trust blindly.

02 / Who it is for

For people who own the result, not just the ticket.

  • Engineers facing an unfamiliar integration, legacy, or production problem.
  • Technical founders and product owners whose tools and data no longer fit the business process.
  • Small teams that need an independent second opinion before a costly or risky change.

03 / Working process

One real problem. One useful outcome.

Only share material you can safely disclose. The goal is a decision and a written next step, not more abstraction.

01

Bring the real problem

A short description, safe code excerpt, sample data, diagram, or relevant logs.

02

Map dependencies

Follow the flow from input to outcome and name every important owner and failure point.

03

Compare trade-offs

Check assumptions and compare options by risk, effort, maintainability, and operations.

04

Leave with next steps

Receive a concise summary with decisions, risks, priorities, and a release or rollback checklist.
